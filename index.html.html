01
Meta Ads · Lead Generation · Premium Interior Brand
Generating quality leads for a high-ticket, design-led product
The challenge: Running Meta Ads for a premium concrete wall panel brand where the audience is niche, the product is high-consideration, and the sales cycle spans multiple steps — ad → lead → call → sales team → close. Impulse buying doesn't happen here.

My approach: Built a two-layer campaign structure. Ran Instagram engagement campaigns first to build brand trust and social proof with the right audience, then activated lead generation campaigns to convert warm traffic. Targeted architects, interior designers, and premium homeowners by profession, interest, and behaviour. Used real project installation visuals as creatives — finished spaces performed significantly better than studio shots.

The funnel: Engagement Ads → Retargeting warm audience → Lead form / WhatsApp CTA → Calling team qualification → Sales team close.

02
Campaign Optimisation · Audience Refinement · 2-Year Journey
Reducing CPL by 25% through smarter audience strategy
The challenge: Early campaigns were generating leads but cost per lead was high and quality was inconsistent. The goal was to reduce CPL while improving the quality of leads entering the sales funnel.

What I did: Analysed which audience segments — architects, interior designers, homeowners, and contractors — were producing leads that actually converted. Eliminated underperforming layers, refined interest stacking, and built custom audiences from past engagers and website visitors. Created lookalike audiences from the strongest lead sources and continuously A/B tested creatives and CTAs over 24 months.

The result: CPL dropped from ₹40–45 when I started to ₹30–35 consistently — a reduction of over 25% — while lead volume was maintained.

03
Creative Strategy · Instagram · Wall Panels & Breeze Blocks
Creative strategy for a highly visual, design-led product
The challenge: Concrete wall panels are a product that buyers need to see in real spaces before they trust it. Generic ad formats don't work. The challenge was developing a creative approach that educates, builds desire, and converts — without looking like a typical ad.

What worked: Real project installation visuals consistently outperformed studio shots. Showing the product installed in finished residential and commercial spaces drove significantly higher engagement and lead intent. For Breeze Blocks, videos showcasing light play through the blocks performed exceptionally well on Instagram Reels and Stories. Lifestyle context always beat product-only shots.

Key learning: For premium, visual products — the creative IS the strategy. Investing in good installation photography directly improved campaign results.

From social media idea to a real stage — event collaboration that put the product in front of thousands

Beyond running ads, I actively think about how a brand can get visibility outside of paid channels. One initiative I proposed was a collaboration with event management companies — using our premium concrete wall panels as the backdrop for concerts and large-scale events.
